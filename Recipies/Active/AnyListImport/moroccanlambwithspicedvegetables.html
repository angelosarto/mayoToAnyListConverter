  1. cut into thin wedges (3.5oz) Beets, cooked, 2 small beet(s)

  7. cut into thin wedges (2.1oz) Red onion, 1 small
  8. 6 oz(s) (6oz) Lamb loin, choice, very lean
  9. 2 tbsp(s) (1.1oz) Red wine vinegar
  10. 2 tbsp(s) (0.1oz) Fresh mint

Prepare Moroccan lamb with spiced vegetables according to recipe instructions below.

Serve half the recipe for dinner and place other half into an airtight container in the fridge for use later in the week.

Moroccan lamb with spiced vegetables instructions

Preheat oven 425°F. Place beets in small roasting pan. Drizzle lightly with a ½ teaspoon of olive oil and season to taste.

In another roasting pan, arrange carrots and Brussels sprouts. Drizzle lightly with a ½ teaspoon of olive oil, sprinkle with 4 teaspoons of the spice mix, and turn to coat. Place both pans into the oven and roast for 20 minutes.

Remove both pans of vegetables from the oven. Add onions to the beets, stir to combine. Roast a further 10 minutes. Remove from oven.

Meanwhile, rub lamb lightly with 1 teaspoon of olive oil and sprinkle with remaining 2 teaspoons spice mix.. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Add lamb, cook for 4 minutes each side, for medium, or until cooked to your liking. Remove to a plate, cover with foil to keep warm. Allow the lamb to stand for 10 minutes before thickly slicing.

Add the beets to the carrots pan. Spoon over the vinegar. Top with the lamb and any pan juices from resting. Scatter over the mint to serve.

TIP: Feel free to swap the lamb for lean beef steak. If you are unable to find Moroccan seasoning in the supermarket, you can make your own using this recipe: Moroccan spice blend.
